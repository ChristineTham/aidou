# Are Emergent Abilities of Large Language Models a Mirage?

> Source: arXiv:2304.15004 (NeurIPS 2023, outstanding paper; PDF in pdf/, fetched 2026-06-30). Rylan Schaeffer, Brando Miranda, Sanmi Koyejo (Stanford).

## Thesis

The cautious counterpoint to "emergence." Argues that the apparent sharp, unpredictable appearance of abilities with scale is often an artefact of the **metric**, not a fundamental change in the model. Discontinuous or all-or-nothing metrics (e.g. exact-match accuracy) turn a smoothly improving underlying capability into an apparent cliff; switch to a continuous metric and the same data shows gradual, predictable improvement. Emergence is, in many measured cases, in the eye of the scorer.

## Use in book

Ch1 ("How prediction becomes reasoning"): keeps the emergence claim honest — capability grows really but "switching on" is partly measurement. Supports the book's cautious-optimism stance.
