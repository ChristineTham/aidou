# Emergent Abilities of Large Language Models

> Source: arXiv:2206.07682 (TMLR 2022; PDF in pdf/, fetched 2026-06-30). Wei, Tay, Bommasani, Raffel, Zoph, Borgeaud, Yogatama, Bosma, Zhou, Metzler, Chi, Hashimoto, Vinyals, Liang, Dean, Fedus.

## Thesis

Defines an *emergent ability* as one absent in smaller models but present in larger ones, and so not predictable by extrapolating small-model performance. Surveys tasks (multi-step arithmetic, transliteration, multi-task understanding) where performance stays near chance until a scale threshold, then rises sharply. Read as evidence that scale unlocks qualitatively new capabilities.
