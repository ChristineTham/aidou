# Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models

> Source: arXiv:2201.11903 (NeurIPS 2022; PDF in pdf/, fetched 2026-06-30). Wei, Wang, Schuurmans, Bosma, Ichter, Xia, Chi, Le, Zhou (Google).

## Thesis

Showing a model a few worked examples that include intermediate reasoning steps (a *chain of thought*) sharply improves performance on arithmetic, commonsense, and symbolic tasks. The ability appears in sufficiently large models and not small ones. Mechanistically, each emitted step becomes context for the next, letting the model spread a hard problem across many small steps instead of one leap.

## Use in book

Ch1 ("How prediction becomes reasoning"): chain-of-thought as the externalisation of serial computation.
