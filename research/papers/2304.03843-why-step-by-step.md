# Why think step by step? Reasoning emerges from the locality of experience

> Source: arXiv:2304.03843 (NeurIPS 2023; PDF in pdf/, fetched 2026-06-30). Ben Prystawski, Michael Y. Li, Noah D. Goodman (Stanford).

## Thesis

Explains *why* chain-of-thought helps a next-token predictor. Hypothesis, tested on controlled data: reasoning is effective when training data consists of **overlapping local clusters** of variables that strongly influence each other. A model trained to predict such data learns reliable *short* hops between closely related variables; chaining those local inferences lets it reach conclusions it cannot produce in a single direct step. Reasoning is thus a consequence of how human experience (and the text recording it) is structured, not a bolted-on faculty.

## Use in book

Ch1 ("How prediction becomes reasoning"): the mechanism that ties reasoning back to prediction — local hops chained into inference.
