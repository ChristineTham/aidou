# Do You Trust ChatGPT? — Perceived Credibility of Human and AI-Generated Content

> Source: arXiv:2309.02524 (5 Sep 2023; PDF in pdf/, fetched 2026-06-30). Huschens, Briesch, Sobania, Rothlauf (Mainz). cs.HC. Recursive cite from "AI is a jester" (Johnson Spink, 2026).

## Thesis

An online survey (N=606 English-speaking participants) testing how content origin and interface presentation affect perceived credibility. Each participant saw four text excerpts across four topics, randomly assigned to one of three UI conditions — **ChatGPT UI, Raw Text UI, or Wikipedia UI** — with each text labelled (in effect) human-generated or LLM-generated, rated on an 11-item message-credibility scale (accurate, authentic, believable, etc.).

Findings:
- Participants attributed **similar credibility** regardless of UI presentation **and** regardless of whether content was human- or AI-generated; no difference in perceived competence or trustworthiness.
- But they rated **AI-generated content as clearer and more engaging**.

Concrete evidence that AI output clears the "feels right" bar (processing fluency) without being judged any less credible — the authors call for more critical evaluation of AI sources.
