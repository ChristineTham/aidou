# Govern the Repository, Not the Agent: Ecosystem-Level Risk in AI-Native Software

> Source: arXiv:2606.28235v1 (26 Jun 2026, full HTML). Daniel Russo. Zenodo 10.5281/zenodo.20759752. 930,292 PRs / 116,211 repos; curated 33,596 PRs / 2,807 repos.

## Thesis

Agents are evaluated one at a time on isolated benchmarks (SWE-bench), but repos accumulate problems no single PR accounts for. Studies *integration friction* via multilevel models: ICC = repo-level variance share = operational test of non-reducibility (emergence).

## Findings

- H1 coupling: 39.7% PRs cross-account engaged; 66.5% repos. H2 locus: conflict flat across agent count (30.8 vs 31.2%) — friction from base-branch churn, not agent interference. H3: resolution-latency ICC 0.50→0.40 after controls, ~3/5 survives; size+age remove 0.7%. H4 specificity: agent ICC 0.30 vs human 0.16 (~2×), holds under task/maturity/merge-path.
- Conflict rate varies by agent: Copilot 15.4% → Codex 32.3%.

## Governance agenda

Assess agents inside target repo; govern change tempo not headcount (merge queue, cap batch, rebase); route review to high-friction paths; track churn/conflict/engagement/ICC. → Ch5: govern repository, not agent.
