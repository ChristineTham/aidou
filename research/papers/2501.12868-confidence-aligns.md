# As Confidence Aligns: Effect of AI Confidence on Human Self-Confidence in Human-AI Decision Making

> Source: arXiv:2501.12868 (22 Jan 2025; PDF in pdf/, fetched 2026-06-30). Jingshu Li et al. CHI '25, DOI 10.1145/3706598.3713336. Open-access arXiv version of the paywalled CHI paper. Recursive cite from "AI is a jester" (Johnson Spink, 2026).

## Thesis

A randomised behavioural experiment on how an AI's expressed confidence reshapes a user's *own* confidence. Findings:

- In human-AI decision making, users' **self-confidence aligns with the AI's confidence** — they become more sure when the AI sounds sure, less when it hesitates.
- This alignment **persists even after the AI is no longer involved**, contaminating later, unaided judgements.
- The alignment **degrades users' confidence calibration** (how well their certainty tracks their actual accuracy).
- **Real-time correctness feedback reduces** the degree of alignment — the one tested condition that helps.

The authors warn that user self-confidence is not independent of AI confidence, a hazard for human-AI collaboration.

## Use in book

Ch2 (The Confidence Trap): primary evidence for confidence contagion that persists after the AI is removed. The real-time-feedback result also supports the book's "verifiable tasks are safer" point — ground-truth feedback is the one reliable antidote.
