# Co-Writing with Opinionated Language Models Affects Users' Views

> Source: arXiv:2302.00560 (1 Feb 2023; PDF in pdf/, fetched 2026-06-30). Jakesch, Bhat, Buschek, Zalmanson, Naaman. CHI '23, DOI 10.1145/3544548.3581196. Open-access arXiv version of the paywalled CHI paper. Recursive cite from "AI is a jester" (Johnson Spink, 2026).

## Thesis

A controlled experiment on whether a biased writing assistant changes not just what people write but what they *believe*. Participants (N=1,506) wrote a post on "is social media good for society"; the treatment group used a GPT-3-powered assistant covertly configured to argue one side. Independent judges (N=500) rated the resulting posts. Using the opinionated assistant shifted the opinions expressed in participants' writing **and** shifted their own attitudes in a subsequent survey. The authors argue the opinions baked into AI writing tools must be monitored and engineered deliberately — a covert "latent persuasion" channel.

## Use in book

Ch2 (The Confidence Trap): evidence that even a low-touch assistant (autocomplete-style) reshapes users' actual beliefs, not just their drafts. Reinforces that the "thinking-partner" framing is not a safe inoculation against AI influence.
