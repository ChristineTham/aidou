# The Expressive Power of Transformers with Chain of Thought

> Source: arXiv:2310.07923 (ICLR 2024; PDF in pdf/, fetched 2026-06-30). William Merrill, Ashish Sabharwal (AI2).

## Thesis

A theoretical result. A standard decoder-only transformer that must answer immediately after reading its input is provably unable to solve some simple problems (e.g. graph connectivity, simulating a finite-state machine). Allowing it to generate intermediate "scratchpad" tokens before answering **genuinely extends its computational power** — the more steps, the larger the class of problems it can solve. Chain-of-thought is therefore real serial computation, not mere stagecraft or prompt theatre.

## Use in book

Ch1 ("How prediction becomes reasoning"): the formal backing that intermediate tokens add computational reach, so CoT reasoning is substantive.
