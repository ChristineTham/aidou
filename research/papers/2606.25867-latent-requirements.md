# LLM-Based Discovery of Latent Requirements from Stakeholder Conversations

> Source: arXiv:2606.25867v1 (24 Jun 2026, full HTML). Sivakumar, Lochner, Nejati, Sabetzadeh. eSentire SOC. Claude Sonnet 4.5. CC BY 4.0.

## Method

LENS: Step1 extract explicit reqs as user stories; Step2 infer latent reqs by reasoning over transcript + org tools/infra; rationale (problem/tool/impl/impact); 13 QUS criteria; timestamp traceability. 12 interviews (~1h, ~9K words, 154 turns).

## Results

- Explicit: best at temp 0.7 — P73/R100/F1 84.4%. Latent: 75% rated moderate/high time-gain by 2 experts (κ=0.54). FPs: manual-work mistaken (66%), existing automation (17%), "right?" confirmation (17%). Feedback: 49% lack tech detail, 22% data/API dependent.
- Latent reqs = elicitation starting points, not implementation-ready.

## Use

Ch2 knowledge-work/Ch3: LLM requirements elicitation; latent need inference + traceability; temperature sweet spot.
