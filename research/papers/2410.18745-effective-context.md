# 2410.18745 — Why Does the Effective Context Length of LLMs Fall Short?

> Source: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.18745> — Chenxin An et al. (24 Oct 2024). PDF in `pdf/2410.18745.pdf`.

## Thesis

A model's *advertised* context window and its *effective* context — the span it can actually use reliably — diverge. The paper attributes the shortfall to a left-skewed position-frequency distribution learned during training (near positions are seen far more often than far ones), so models under-use long-range positions; the effective length often lands well short of the maximum (around half in practice). Proposes a remedy (ShifTed Rotray position embeddING, "STRING") that reweights position indices at inference to lift long-context performance.
