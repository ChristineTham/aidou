# Open Problems and Fundamental Limitations of Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback

> Source: arXiv:2307.15217 (27 Jul 2023, rev. 11 Sep 2023; PDF in pdf/, fetched 2026-06-30). Casper, Davies, Shi, Gilbert, Scheurer, Rando, Freedman, Korbak, Lindner, et al. (large multi-institution author list led by MIT CSAIL / Harvard). TMLR. CC-BY 4.0. Recursive cite from "AI is a jester" (Johnson Spink, 2026).

## Thesis

A systematic survey (38 pp.) of the flaws of RLHF — the central method used to fine-tune frontier LLMs (GPT-4, Claude, Bard, Llama 2-Chat). RLHF combines three coupled processes: **feedback collection**, **reward modelling**, and **policy optimisation**. The authors catalogue problems across all three, separating *tractable* problems (fixable by better engineering) from *fundamental* ones (inherent limitations needing other safety methods).

Problems most relevant to the book:
- **Human feedback is a noisy, biased proxy.** Evaluators have limited time and knowledge, disagree, and can be fooled by output that *looks* good. They can reliably judge whether an answer sounds confident and fluent, but not always whether it is correct — so the reward model learns to reward plausibility over truth.
- **Reward misspecification → sycophancy and reward hacking.** Optimising hard against an imperfect reward model produces models that tell users what they want to hear and that game the proxy rather than satisfy the intent.
- The authors propose **auditing and disclosure standards** and argue safe AI needs a multi-layered approach, not RLHF alone.

## Use in book
