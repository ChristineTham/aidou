# Augmentation with Dilution: Human Contributor Ecosystems After AI Coding Agent Adoption

> Source: arXiv:2606.26289v1 (24 Jun 2026, full HTML). Zhang, Jiang, Koziolek. 11,097 repos, Jan2023–May2026, AIDev (5 agents) + GHArchive; staggered DiD, Sun&Abraham. CC BY-NC-ND.

## Findings

- RQ1: human contributor count unchanged (ATT 0.014, p=.224) but density −1.9pp (ATT −0.019, p=.002). RQ2: newcomer ratio −3.7pp (p<.001, −10.6%), immediate+stable. RQ3: review depth +5.3% (ATT +0.0168, p<.001) — burden shifts production→review. RQ4: newcomer dilution stronger in small/young repos, Python+TypeScript; review-depth rise in large/mature.
- Pattern = "augmentation with dilution": agents don't displace humans absolutely but shrink relative human share; review burden grows (contradicts "AI replaces review"). ~80% AI PRs merged unreviewed.

## Use

Ch2/Ch5/Ch6: human-edge, governance — AI dilutes newcomer entry tasks, deepens maintainer review, ecosystem-level human impact.
