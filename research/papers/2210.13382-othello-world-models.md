# Emergent World Representations: Exploring a Sequence Model Trained on a Synthetic Task

> Source: arXiv:2210.13382 (ICLR 2023; PDF in pdf/, fetched 2026-06-30). Kenneth Li, Aspen Hopkins, David Bau, Fernanda Viégas, Hanspeter Pfister, Martin Wattenberg. "Othello-GPT".

## Thesis

Trains a GPT variant only to predict legal next moves in the board game Othello, with no rules or board supplied. Probing reveals an **emergent internal representation of the board state** — and interventional edits to that representation steer the model's outputs, proving it is *used*, not incidental. Evidence that next-token prediction, at scale, induces a model of the process generating the sequence rather than mere surface statistics.

## Use in book

Ch1 (Capabilities & Limitations → "How prediction becomes reasoning"): the cleanest demonstration that prediction forces internal world-models.
