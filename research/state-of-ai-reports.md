# State of AI Reports — Summary

PDFs in this folder. Compiled 29 Jun 2026. Figures cited from each report.

## Stanford HAI — 2026 AI Index ([report](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2026-ai-index-report))
- Capability accelerating: industry made 90%+ of notable frontier models; SWE-bench Verified 60%→~100% in a year. Adoption 88%; 4/5 students use GenAI.
- US–China gap effectively closed (top model leads ~2.7%). US: 5,427 data centres; TSMC fabs nearly all leading chips.
- Jagged frontier: IMO gold, but reads clocks 50.1%; OSWorld 12%→~66%. Responsible AI lags — incidents 233→362. US private AI investment $285.9B. GenAI 53% adoption in 3 yrs. Experts vs public 50-pt optimism gap.

## McKinsey — State of AI 2025 ([article](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai)) → `the-state-of-ai-in-2025-vf.pdf`
- 88% regular AI use; ~1/3 scaling. 62% experimenting with agents, 23% scaling. Only 39% report any EBIT impact; ~6% are high performers.
- High performers redesign workflows, set growth/innovation goals, human-validation processes. 51% saw a negative consequence; mitigate ~4 risks. 32% expect headcount cuts.

## Deloitte — State of AI in Enterprise 2026 ([page](https://www.deloitte.com/au/en/issues/generative-ai/state-of-ai-in-enterprise.html)) → `deloitte-state-of-ai-2026.pdf`
- 69% AU use autonomous agents; only 22% have advanced agent governance. 25% globally say GenAI transforming business. 72% weigh vendor country-of-origin (sovereign AI). Pilots→production still the gap.

## stateof.ai — State of AI 2025 (Benaich) → `State of AI Report - 2025 ONLINE.pdf`
- OpenAI narrow frontier lead; China (#2) closing. Reasoning + RL the year's theme. 44% US businesses pay for AI (5% in 2023); 95% practitioners use AI. Power is the new compute constraint; safety budgets < a lab's daily burn.
