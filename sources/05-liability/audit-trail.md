## Industry uses
In [telecommunications](/wiki/Telecommunications "Telecommunications"), the term means a record of both completed and attempted accesses and service, or [data](/wiki/Data "Data") forming a logical path linking a [sequence](/wiki/Sequence "Sequence") of events, used to trace the transactions that have affected the contents of a record.
In [accounting](/wiki/Accounting "Accounting"), it refers to [documentation](/wiki/Documentation "Documentation") of detailed transactions supporting summary [ledger](/wiki/Ledger "Ledger") entries. This documentation may be on paper or on electronic records.
In [clinical research](/wiki/Clinical_research "Clinical research"), server based systems such as [clinical trial management systems](/wiki/Clinical_trial_management_system "Clinical trial management system") (CTMS) require audit trails. Anything regulatory or [QA/QC](/wiki/QA/QC "QA/QC") related also requires audit trails.
In [voting](/wiki/Voting "Voting"), a [voter-verified paper audit trail](/wiki/Voter-verified_paper_audit_trail "Voter-verified paper audit trail") is a method of providing feedback to voters using a [ballotless](/wiki/Ballot "Ballot") voting system.
