## History
Artisans were the dominant producers of primary [products](/wiki/Product_(business) "Product (business)") before the [Industrial Revolution](/wiki/Industrial_Revolution "Industrial Revolution").
### Medieval artisans
During the [Middle Ages](/wiki/Middle_Ages "Middle Ages"), the term "artisan" was applied to those who made things or provided services. It did not apply to unskilled [manual labourers](/wiki/Manual_labour "Manual labour"). Artisans were divided into two distinct groups: those who operated their own businesses and those who did not. The former were called [masters](/wiki/Master_craftsman "Master craftsman"), while the latter were the [journeymen](/wiki/Journeyman "Journeyman") and [apprentices](/wiki/Apprenticeship "Apprenticeship").
## Shokunin
The related verb *kiwameru*<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span title="Japanese-language text"><span lang="ja">極める / 究める</span></span>, <span title="Hepburn transliteration">*"to bring to the extreme"*</span><span style="margin-left:.09em">)</span></span> is closely associated with this ideal. Where *shokunin* describes the identity and social role of the practitioner, *kiwameru* describes the orientation of their effort — a ceaseless pursuit of the absolute limit of a skill, understood as a direction rather than a fixed destination.
