Chess with computer assistance

"Centaur (chess)" redirects here. For the fairy chess piece, see [List of fairy chess pieces § Centaur](/wiki/List_of_fairy_chess_pieces#Centaur "List of fairy chess pieces").
**Advanced chess** is a form of [chess](/wiki/Chess "Chess") in which each human player uses a [computer chess](/wiki/Computer_chess "Computer chess") [engine](/wiki/Chess_engine "Chess engine") to explore the possible results of candidate moves. With this computer assistance, the human player controls and decides the game.
- increasing the level of play to heights never before seen in chess;
- offering the public an overview of the mental processes of strong human chess players and powerful chess computers, and the combination of their forces.

During the match, the players will typically form [strategic](/wiki/Chess_strategy "Chess strategy") plans in their minds, then enter the candidate sequences of moves into the computer to analyze and make sure there are no [blunders](/wiki/Blunder_(chess) "Blunder (chess)") and other possible holes. After seeing the computer's analysis, the human player will compare each candidate sequence's merits and may even introduce a new variation if time permits. The player will typically play out the move he or she has established (with computer help) to be strongest. If there are two or more moves which the computer considers to be of equal strength (such situations are frequent), the human player will use their own strategic skills, experience, and analytical judgment capabilities to decide which move to play. The humans are in charge during the whole match, and are formally free to play any moves they consider the best, at their own discretion. During the [opening](/wiki/Chess_opening "Chess opening"), the players may consult a large database of opening moves and variations, containing information about who played a particular variation, when it was played, and with what success. However, a particular tournament's rules may prohibit using databases in such a manner.
During the whole game, the players' [computer monitors](/wiki/Computer_monitor "Computer monitor") are projected onto large screens, making it possible for the viewing audience to watch how the strongest players decide their moves and make their plans. Typically, there will be a commentator in a separate room, equipped with the identical hardware as players, who will provide commentary to the audience—this way, the audience is given real insight into the thought processes of the strongest players.

## Online tournaments
<figcaption>Eros Riccio</figcaption>
</figure>
There were, therefore, a number of advanced chess tournaments online, called centaur freestyle chess tournaments.
ChessBase organized the Computer Bild Spiele Schach Turnier for the 2008 Dresden Chess Olympics, which the Italian Eros Riccio won.
The [Internet Chess Club](/wiki/Internet_Chess_Club "Internet Chess Club") also held its own freestyle tour dubbed as 1st Ultimate Chess Championship 2015, won by Alvin Alcala.
