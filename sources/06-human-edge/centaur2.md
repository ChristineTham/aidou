### India
In a popular legend associated with [Pazhaya Sreekanteswaram Temple](/wiki/Pazhaya_Sreekanteswaram_Temple "Pazhaya Sreekanteswaram Temple") in [Thiruvananthapuram](/wiki/Thiruvananthapuram "Thiruvananthapuram"), the curse of a saintly [Brahmin](/wiki/Brahmin "Brahmin") transformed a handsome [Yadava](/wiki/Yadava "Yadava") prince into a creature having a horse's body and the prince's head, arms, and torso in place of the head and neck of the horse.
### Russia
A centaur-like half-human, half-equine creature called *[Polkan](/wiki/Polkan "Polkan")* appeared in Russian folk art and [lubok](/wiki/Lubok "Lubok") prints of the 17th–19th centuries. Polkan is originally based on *Pulicane*, a half-dog from [Andrea da Barberino](/wiki/Andrea_da_Barberino "Andrea da Barberino")'s poem *I Reali di Francia*, which was once popular in the Slavonic world in prose translations.
## Artistic representations
### Classical art
</figure>
In [Greek art](/wiki/Ancient_Greek_art "Ancient Greek art") of the [Archaic period](/wiki/Archaic_Greece#Art "Archaic Greece"), centaurs are depicted in three different forms.
- Some centaurs are depicted with a human torso attached to the body of a horse at the [withers](/wiki/Withers "Withers"), where the horse's neck would be; this form, designated "Class A" by Professor Paul Baur, later became standard.
- "Class B" centaurs are depicted with a human body and legs joined at the waist to the hindquarters of a horse; in some cases centaurs of both Class A and Class B appear together.

Other hybrid creatures appear in Greek mythology, always with some liminal connection that links Hellenic culture with archaic or non-Hellenic cultures:
- [Furietti Centaurs](/wiki/Furietti_Centaurs "Furietti Centaurs") – Pair of Hellenistic or Roman statues
- [Ichthyocentaur](/wiki/Ichthyocentaur "Ichthyocentaur") – A type of aquatic centaur with a fish tail instead of hindquarters.
- [Ipotane](/wiki/Ipotane "Ipotane") – Another half-human half-horse creature.
- [Legendary creature](/wiki/Legendary_creature "Legendary creature") – Supernatural animal
- [Lists of legendary creatures](/wiki/Lists_of_legendary_creatures "Lists of legendary creatures")
- [Minotaur](/wiki/Minotaur "Minotaur") – Creature of Greek mythology
- [Onocentaur](/wiki/Onocentaur "Onocentaur") – A type of centaur that is part-donkey.
- [Sagittarius](/wiki/Sagittarius_(astrology) "Sagittarius (astrology)") – Ninth astrological sign of the zodiac
- [Sagittarius (constellation)](/wiki/Sagittarius_(constellation) "Sagittarius (constellation)") – Zodiac constellation in the southern celestial hemisphere
- [Satyr](/wiki/Satyr "Satyr") – Male nature spirit with horse or goat features found in Greek mythology
Also,
- Hindu [Kamadhenu](/wiki/Kamadhenu "Kamadhenu")
- Indian [Kinnara](/wiki/Kinnara_kingdom "Kinnara kingdom") which are half-horse and half-man creatures.
- Islamic [Buraq](/wiki/Buraq "Buraq"), a heavenly steed often portrayed as an equine being with a human face.
- Philippine [Tikbalang](/wiki/Tikbalang "Tikbalang")
- Roman [Faun](/wiki/Faun "Faun"), and the [Hippopodes](/wiki/Hippopodes "Hippopodes") of [Pomponius Mela](/wiki/Pomponius_Mela "Pomponius Mela"), [Pliny the Elder](/wiki/Pliny_the_Elder "Pliny the Elder"), and later authors.
- Welsh [Ceffyl Dŵr](/wiki/Ceffyl_D%C5%B5r "Ceffyl Dŵr")
Additionally, *[Bucentaur](/wiki/Bucentaur "Bucentaur")*, the name of several historically important [Venetian](/wiki/Republic_of_Venice "Republic of Venice") vessels, was linked to a posited ox-centaur or *βουκένταυρος* *(boukentauros)* by fanciful and likely spurious folk-etymology.
