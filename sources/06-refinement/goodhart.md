## Priority and background
<figcaption>Charles Goodhart, for whom the adage is named, delivering a speech in 2012</figcaption>
</figure>
Shortly after Goodhart's publication, others suggested closely related ideas, including the [Lucas critique](/wiki/Lucas_critique "Lucas critique") (1976). As applied in [economics](/wiki/Economics "Economics"), the law is also implicit in the idea of [rational expectations](/wiki/Rational_expectations "Rational expectations"), an economic theory that those aware of a system of rewards and punishments will optimize their actions within that system to achieve their desired results. For example, if an employee is rewarded based on the number of cars sold each month, they will try to sell more cars, even at a loss.
And suggested a [corollary](/wiki/Corollary "Corollary") for use in [financial risk modelling](/wiki/Financial_risk_modeling "Financial risk modeling"):
> All metrics of scientific evaluation are bound to be abused. Goodhart's law \[...\] states that when a feature of the economy is picked as an indicator of the economy, then it inexorably ceases to function as that indicator because people start to game it.

## See also
- [Campbell's law](/wiki/Campbell%27s_law "Campbell's law") – "The more any quantitative social indicator is used for social decision-making, the more subject it will be to corruption pressures"
- [Confirmation bias](/wiki/Confirmation_bias "Confirmation bias") – the tendency to search for and recall information that confirms or supports one's prior beliefs
- [Lucas critique](/wiki/Lucas_critique "Lucas critique") – the observation that it is naive to try to predict the effects of a change in economic policy entirely based on relationships observed in historical data
- [Map–territory relation](/wiki/Map%E2%80%93territory_relation "Map–territory relation") – a type of [reification fallacy](/wiki/Reification_(fallacy) "Reification (fallacy)") where a [model](/wiki/Mathematical_model "Mathematical model") is confused with the thing being modeled
- [McNamara fallacy](/wiki/McNamara_fallacy "McNamara fallacy") – ignoring qualitative metrics on the basis that they cannot be measured
- [Metric fixation](/wiki/Metric_fixation "Metric fixation") – Tendency for decision-makers to place excessively large emphases on selected metrics
- [Peter principle](/wiki/Peter_principle "Peter principle") – individuals are promoted based on success in their previous roles, and not the role of the new position
- [Reification (fallacy)](/wiki/Reification_(fallacy) "Reification (fallacy)") – Fallacy of treating an abstraction as if it were a real thing
- [Reflexivity (social theory)](/wiki/Reflexivity_(social_theory) "Reflexivity (social theory)") – Circular relationships between cause and effect
- [Reward hacking](/wiki/Reward_hacking "Reward hacking") – occurs when artificial intelligence optimizes a poorly specified reward without reaching the intended outcome
- [Surrogation](/wiki/Surrogation "Surrogation") – in business, when a measure of a construct of interest evolves to replace that construct
