## Core concepts
## History
## Recognizing the effects of extending processing power
[Andy Hopper](/wiki/Andy_Hopper "Andy Hopper") from Cambridge University UK proposed and demonstrated the concept of "Teleporting" – where applications follow the user wherever he/she moves.
[Roy Want](/wiki/Roy_Want "Roy Want") (now at Google), while at Olivetti Research Ltd, designed the first "Active Badge System", which is an advanced location computing system where personal mobility is merged with computing. Later at Xerox PARC, he designed and built the "PARCTab" or simply "Tab", widely recognized as the world's first Context-Aware computer, which has great similarity to the modern smartphone.
Bill Schilit (now at Google) also did some earlier work in this topic, and participated in the early Mobile Computing workshop held in Santa Cruz in 1996.
## Examples
[Ambient Devices](/wiki/Ambient_Devices "Ambient Devices") has produced an "orb", a "dashboard", and a "[weather beacon](/wiki/Weather_beacon "Weather beacon")": these decorative devices receive data from a [wireless network](/wiki/Wireless_network "Wireless network") and report current events, such as stock prices and the weather, like the [Nabaztag](/wiki/Nabaztag "Nabaztag"), which was invented by [Rafi Haladjian](/wiki/Rafi_Haladjian "Rafi Haladjian") and [Olivier Mével](/wiki/Olivier_M%C3%A9vel "Olivier Mével"), and manufactured by the company Violet.
Ubiquitous computing research has focused on building an environment in which computers allow humans to focus attention on select aspects of the environment and operate in supervisory and policy-making roles. Ubiquitous computing emphasizes the creation of a human computer interface that can interpret and support a user's intentions.
